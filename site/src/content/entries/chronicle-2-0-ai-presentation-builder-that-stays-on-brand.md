**What it is.** A web-based presentation tool that uses AI to turn a prompt, a set of notes, or an existing deck into a finished slide presentation. It asks you a few questions up front about tone and brand, generates a first draft, and then lets you keep shaping it through back-and-forth conversation instead of digging through menus.

**Why it matters.** Most AI deck tools either spit out something generic that takes longer to fix than to rebuild, or they hand you a blank canvas and call that "AI-powered." Chronicle is aiming at the space in between: a draft that actually reflects your brand and content, with a conversational editing loop so you are not hunting through formatting panels to change one thing.

**How to use it.** Start with whatever you have: a brief, a set of bullet points, or an existing deck you want to rework. Paste it in, answer the setup questions about your brand and audience, and let it build the first draft. From there, treat the conversation pane like a creative collaborator. Ask it to tighten the narrative on a slide, swap the layout, or shift the tone. When you are close, you can go in and adjust individual slides directly. The workflow is probably most useful in the early stages of a project, when you need a credible starting point fast rather than a pixel-perfect final.

**Limits.** How well it holds to your brand depends on how clearly you can describe it up front. If your visual identity is complex or tightly controlled, you will likely need real refinement passes after the AI draft. The conversational editing is only as precise as what you can articulate in words, so fiddly layout adjustments may still be faster done by hand. And like any AI presentation tool, the output can drift toward a certain polished-but-safe aesthetic that needs creative intervention to feel genuinely distinctive.
