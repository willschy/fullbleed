**What it is.** Pitch Agent is built into Pitch, the collaborative presentation tool. You give it a prompt and any files you want to draw from, and it generates slides. The difference it is claiming over other AI slide tools is that it builds from your existing template and image style rather than applying your brand colors to a generic structure.

**Why it matters.** Most AI presentation tools produce something that looks branded at a glance but falls apart the moment a real designer looks at it. The layouts are borrowed, the type is off, and you spend as much time fixing slides as you would have making them. If Pitch Agent is genuinely working from your template, that is a different proposition: something closer to a first draft you can actually hand off, rather than a starting point you have to rebuild.

**How to use it.** Start in Pitch with a template your team already uses. Pull up the Agent, describe what the deck needs, and attach any relevant files, a brief, a strategy doc, a competitor teardown. It generates the slides and you talk back to it in chat to refine. That chat-based refinement is where it earns its keep: instead of hunting through menus, you tell it to tighten the hierarchy on slide four or swap the image direction, and it handles the edit. The workflow lives entirely inside Pitch, so collaboration and commenting stay in the same place.

**Limits.** How well it holds to your design language depends entirely on how well your template is built. A loose or inconsistent template will produce loose, inconsistent output. The chat refinement also has a ceiling: it can adjust and iterate, but a deck that needs real art direction is still going to need a real art director. And because Agent lives inside Pitch, you are committing to that ecosystem. If your team works in Figma or Google Slides, this does not cross over.
